A Quick Guide to King Street

Charleston's main thoroughfare for shopping, dining, and history, split into three walkable districts. Here's how to make sense of it in one visit.

Three Districts, One Street

Lower, Middle & Upper King

Lower King — The Antiques District

Broad Street to Market Street

The oldest stretch of King Street, lined with antique shops, art galleries, and historic architecture that make it feel like a living museum. George C. Birlant & Co. is one of the most recognized antique dealers here, and the annual Charleston Antiques Show draws collectors from across the country. Stop into Millers All Day for a classic Charleston breakfast before you start browsing.

Middle King — The Fashion District

Market Street to Calhoun Street

The retail heart of King Street, anchored by Marion Square. Expect a mix of national retailers like Anthropologie, Kate Spade, and J. Crew alongside local boutiques such as Hampden Clothing and Candlefish. On the second Sunday of most months, this stretch closes to traffic for a pedestrian-only event with outdoor dining and street performers.

Upper King — The Design & Dining District

Calhoun Street to Spring Street

The newest and trendiest section, packed with some of the city's best-regarded restaurants, including The Ordinary, Felix Cocktails et Cuisine, and The Darling Oyster Bar. It's also home to live music venues like the Charleston Music Hall and Music Farm, plus a growing lineup of independent boutiques for artisan goods and home decor.

Before You Go

Quick Tips

Walk, Don't Drive

Parking is limited and metered spots are hard to find on weekends. King Street is easiest to enjoy on foot.

Start at Marion Square

It's the natural dividing line between Middle and Upper King, and hosts the Charleston Farmers Market on Saturdays in season.

Pace Yourself

The full street runs roughly a mile and a half. Pick one district to focus on rather than trying to cover all three in one outing.
